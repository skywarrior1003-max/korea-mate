Korea has one of the world's best transportation networks for tourists — extensive, punctual, clean, and affordable. Once you understand the system, getting between cities and navigating within them becomes completely effortless.

## The T-money Card: Your Korea Travel Companion

The **T-money card** (티머니) is a rechargeable transit card that works on:
- All Seoul Metro lines
- Busan, Daegu, Incheon, Gwangju subway systems
- Intercity and local buses nationwide
- Taxis in most major cities
- Convenience stores (GS25, CU, 7-Eleven, Emart24)

### Getting a T-money Card

Buy at any subway station ticket machine, convenience store, or airport upon arrival.
- **Card cost:** ₩3,000–₩5,000 (non-refundable)
- **Minimum load:** ₩1,000
- **Reload:** At any convenience store or subway station

### T-money Discount

Using T-money gives a **₩100 discount per ride** vs paying cash on buses. Over 10 days of daily transit use, this adds up.

### Kakao Pay / Samsung Pay T-money

If your phone supports NFC, you can add a virtual T-money card to Kakao Pay (Android) or Samsung Wallet. This eliminates carrying a physical card.

## KTX: High-Speed Rail Between Cities

**KTX** (Korea Train eXpress) connects Korea's major cities at up to 330km/h. It's the fastest and most comfortable way to travel between Seoul, Busan, Daegu, Daejeon, and Gwangju.

### Key Routes & Times

| Route | Duration | Price (Standard) |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| Seoul → Busan | 2hr 15min | ₩59,800 |
| Seoul → Daejeon | 50min | ₩23,700 |
| Seoul → Daegu | 1hr 40min | ₩43,500 |
| Seoul → Gwangju | 1hr 33min | ₩46,800 |
| Seoul → Gyeongju | 2hr 10min | ₩53,400 |

### Booking KTX

1. **KORAIL website** (letskorail.com) — English interface, accepts international Visa/Mastercard
2. **KORAIL app** — iOS and Android, English language available
3. **At stations** — ticket machines have English mode; manned windows available

**Tip:** Book at least 1 week ahead on weekends and public holidays. First-class (KTX-Sancheon) upgrades are worth it for the extra legroom (~₩10,000 more).

### Rail Pass Options

The **Korea Rail Pass (KR Pass)** allows unlimited KTX travel for 3, 5, or 7 consecutive days. Available only for foreign visitors; purchase before arriving in Korea or at major KTX stations.

- 3-day pass: ~₩182,000
- 5-day pass: ~₩240,000
- **Worth buying if:** You plan 3+ inter-city train trips

## Seoul Metro: The World's Best Subway

Seoul's metro system has 9 numbered lines + several suburban and express lines covering the entire capital region. It's the backbone of Seoul transit.

### Key Facts
- **Operating hours:** ~05:30–00:30 (varies by line and station)
- **Fare:** ₩1,400–₩1,800 (distance-based), ₩100 cheaper with T-money
- **Frequency:** Every 2–5 minutes during rush hour, every 5–10 minutes off-peak
- **Language:** English announcements and signage on all lines
- **Wi-Fi:** Free on most trains and all platforms

### Essential Lines for Tourists

| Line | Color | Key Stations |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Line 1 | Dark Blue | Seoul Station (KTX), Dongdaemun |
| Line 2 | Green | Hongdae, Gangnam, Sindorim |
| Line 3 | Orange | Gyeongbokgung, Anguk, Apgujeong |
| Line 4 | Light Blue | Myeongdong, Seoul Station, Dongdaemun |
| Line 9 | Gold | Express to Gimpo Airport, COEX |
| AREX | Red | Incheon Airport → Seoul Station (43 min) |

### Naver Maps for Seoul Transit

**Google Maps works** for Seoul subway, but **Naver Maps** is more accurate for bus routes, walking directions, and real-time transit updates. Set the language to English in settings.

## Intercity Buses (시외버스 / 고속버스)

For cities not well-served by KTX (like Gyeongju, Jeonju, Gangneung), intercity buses are the best option.

### Bus Types

- **Express Bus (고속버스):** Fastest option, no intermediate stops. Seoul Express Bus Terminal (강남 고속버스터미널, Line 3/7/9)
- **Intercity Bus (시외버스):** More stops, broader coverage. Departs from various terminals.

### Key Routes by Bus

| Route | Duration | Price |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Seoul → Gyeongju | ~3hr 20min | ₩26,000 |
| Seoul → Jeonju | ~2hr 30min | ₩14,500 |
| Seoul → Gangneung | ~2hr 20min | ₩18,700 |
| Busan → Gyeongju | ~50min | ₩4,900 |

### Booking Buses

Book online at **kobus.co.kr** (English available) or at the terminal. Buses typically run every 30–60 minutes on major routes.

## Incheon Airport Transit

Incheon International Airport (ICN) is 50km west of Seoul. Three options to reach the city:

### AREX (Airport Express)
- **Express Train:** Incheon → Seoul Station in **43 minutes** (₩9,500, no stops)
- **All-stop train:** ~66 minutes, stops at Hongdae, City Airport station, etc. (₩4,950)
- Trains run every 5–10 minutes from 05:23 to 00:16

### Airport Limousine Bus
- Routes to Gangnam, Hongdae, Insadong, and major hotel areas
- Journey time: 60–90 minutes (traffic-dependent)
- Fare: ₩9,000–₩17,000

### Taxi
- Regular taxi to central Seoul: ₩70,000–₩100,000 (1 hour in normal traffic)
- Kakao Black (premium): ~₩110,000–₩140,000

## Gimpo Airport (GMP): Domestic Flights

Gimpo Airport (20 min west of central Seoul) handles:
- **Domestic routes:** Seoul → Jeju, Seoul → Busan, Seoul → Yeosu
- **International:** Seoul → Tokyo Haneda, Seoul → Osaka Itami, Seoul → Beijing, Taipei

**From city:** Line 5 or Line 9 subway (30–40 min from central Seoul).

## Rental Cars in Korea

Recommended **only** for:
- Jeju Island (essential)
- Rural countryside exploration
- Groups of 3+ where per-person cost is competitive

**For cities:** Driving in Seoul and Busan is not recommended for tourists due to traffic, one-way systems, and paid parking (₩2,000–₩5,000/hour).

**Car rental:** Lotte Rent-a-Car, KT Kumho, SKR available at major airports. International Driving Permit required (obtain in your home country before departure).
